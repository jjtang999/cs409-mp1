Legendary Career

New England Patriots

Tom Brady spent 20 seasons with the New England Patriots, leading the team to an unprecedented era of success with 6 Super Bowl championships. Drafted in the sixth round of the 2000 NFL Draft, Brady became the starter in 2001 and led the Patriots to their first Super Bowl victory that same season.

During his time with the Patriots, Brady established himself as one of the greatest quarterbacks in NFL history, setting numerous records and consistently leading his team to the playoffs.

Tampa Bay Buccaneers

In 2020, Brady signed with the Tampa Bay Buccaneers, bringing his talents to a new team after two decades with the Patriots. In his first season with the Buccaneers, Brady led the team to a Super Bowl victory, earning his 7th championship ring.

This historic achievement solidified Brady's status as the greatest quarterback of all time, proving he could succeed outside of the Patriots' system and contributing to his legacy as the most accomplished player in NFL history.

Retirement & Legacy

After announcing his retirement in February 2022, Brady shocked the world by returning for another season with the Buccaneers. Following the 2022 season, Brady announced his retirement "for good," ending an illustrious 23-year NFL career.

His legacy extends beyond his on-field achievements, with ventures including the TB12 Method, a holistic approach to health and wellness, and various business endeavors. He has since transitioned into broadcasting, signing a deal to become a lead NFL analyst.
